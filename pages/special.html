Special Mechanics

Squares

There are 4 types of squares. Landing on a square will trigger its effect.

Blank Square

Indicated by a blank or empty white/yellow square. Landing on this square does nothing.

Q & A Square

Indicated by a square with a blue question mark. Players who landed on this square will be given a question to answer.

Spin Square

Indicated by a symbol on the square. Players landing on this square will have to spin the 'Cyclo Wheel'

Wall/Blocked Square

Indicated by a red X on a gray square. When moving, if this square is in the way, the square will block the players movement.

Players cannot land or pass through this square.

Start Square

Indicated by a green square with a house on it. All palyers start on this square.

Gain/Lose Elctron Square

Indicated by a + or - sign next to a number with the colors green/red respectively.

If the player lands on a Gain Square (+) They will gain the amount of electrons and add it to their orbitals.

If the player lands on a Lose Square (-) They will lose the amount of electrons and subtract it from their orbitals.

Electrons and Orbitals

Each player will have boxes where they can place their electrons.

Each box can only contain 2 electrons.

Q & A

Landing on a Q & A square will force to palyer to answer a question in Science 9 Quater 2.

There are 3 levels of questions indicated by its color:

  • Red - Difficult Question - 6 Electrons
  • Yellow - Intermediate Question - 4 Electrons
  • Green - Easy Question - 2 Electrons

If a player answers a question correctly, the will gain the amount of electrons indicated.

If a player answers incorrectly, they will return back to the square they came from.

Cyclo Spin

If a player lands on a 'Cyclo Spin' square they will have to spin the 'Cyclo Wheel'.

  • Lose All - The player will lose all the electrons in their orbital.
  • +20 - The player will gain an additional 20 electrons to their orbital.
